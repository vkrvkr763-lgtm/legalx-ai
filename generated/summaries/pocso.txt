The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO Act) aims to safeguard children under 18 from sexual abuse, harassment, and pornography.

**Purpose:** Its main goal is to protect children from various sexual offenses, ensure their well-being, and establish special courts for quick, sensitive trials. It prioritizes a child's privacy and confidentiality throughout the legal process.

**Offenses and Penalties:** The Act defines several crimes:
*   **Serious Sexual Assault (Penetrative):** Punishments range from 10 years to life imprisonment. Aggravated cases (e.g., by authority figures, relatives, or causing death) can lead to 20 years to life, or even death.
*   **Sexual Assault:** Other sexual acts, carrying jail terms from 3 to 5 years, with higher penalties for aggravated forms.
*   **Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual acts or words, punishable by up to 3 years in jail.
*   **Child Pornography:** Using a child for or storing pornographic material, with penalties from 3 to 7 years.
*   Penalties are higher if the offender is in a position of trust, part of a gang, or if the child is very young or severely harmed.

**Rights and Protections for Children:**
*   **Special Courts:** Cases are heard in dedicated courts with Special Public Prosecutors.
*   **Child-Friendly Procedures:** Statements are recorded sensitively; children are protected from seeing the accused during testimony. Trials are private.
*   **Support:** Children have the right to legal aid, interpreters, and expert assistance.
*   **Compensation:** Victims receive financial support for medical expenses and rehabilitation.

**Reporting and Accountability:**
*   Anyone aware of such an offense must report it. Failure to report is punishable. Making a false complaint is also an offense.
