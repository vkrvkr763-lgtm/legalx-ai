This Act, called **The National Forensic Sciences University Act, 2020**, establishes the National Forensic Sciences University (NFSU) as a crucial institution of national importance in India.

**Purpose:**
Its primary goal is to become a center of excellence for teaching, training, and research in forensic science, applied behavioral science, law, criminology, and related technologies. The University aims to strengthen criminal justice systems by developing expertise, promoting innovation, and coordinating with government bodies to improve crime investigation and prevention. It was formed by merging the Gujarat Forensic Sciences University and the Lok Nayak Jayaprakash Narayan National Institute of Criminology and Forensic Sciences.

**Rights:**
The Act ensures the University is open to all individuals, regardless of their race, creed, or social class. Students have the right to a fair process for addressing grievances, such as being debarred from exams or facing disciplinary action, through a special Tribunal of Arbitration.

**Protections:**
Importantly, the NFSU is considered a 'public authority' under the Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005. This means ordinary citizens can request information from the University, promoting transparency and accountability. Existing employees and students from the merged institutions also have their service conditions and academic progress protected during the transition.

**Penalties:**
This Act primarily focuses on establishing and governing the University. It does not outline any specific penalties or fines for ordinary citizens. Disciplinary actions against students are internal university matters, handled through the established redressal mechanisms.
